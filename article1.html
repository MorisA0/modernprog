Modern Progressive Metal Pioneers: Monuments

12/05/2022

I found out about modern metal when I stumbled upon an EMG pickup demo video by John Browne. He played a piece called “I, The Creator”. I have never encountered that playing style before so I was awestruck with the music I was hearing. It was heavy and distorted, but at the same time precise and restrained. A combination of order and chaos. I always remembered his name and was captivated with his playing technique.

Focusing on left-hand dexterity is commonplace among guitar players. As a player, I was initially inspired by jazz/rock fusion guitar. Building jazz and blues vocabulary; playing and improvising fast runs cleanly and coherently.

Before I discovered modern progressive metal, this was my main focus. John Browne of Monuments’ impressive right-hand technique gave me an epiphany that there are a lot of dimensions to music especially when it comes to playing and composing. The music of Monuments has influenced many guitar players and I am definitely one of them. I firmly believe that this group has largely contributed to the evolution of modern progressive metal.

John Browne also founded RiffHard, a company that specializes in teaching rhythm guitars.
