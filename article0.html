Brief History and Definition of “Djent”

12/04/2022

When a musical genre is created, there aren’t really any governing musical academic bodies that regulate it. A musical genre is just born out of fusion of other forms of music and along the process, creating a community of enthusiasts.

“Djent” is the sound of a heavy, distorted, and palm-muted playing of a guitar. It is an onomatopoeia that was taken out of context when Misha Mansoor, (aka Bulb, also a member of Periphery) was looking for guitar pickups. During his early rise, he had quite a following and most likely some of them thought it was a genre that he plays.

Well-known metal artists frown upon this misconception. Most of them say that there is no such genre as “Djent”. Regardless, this has caught on and it created a thriving community. With the evolving technology, many artists are flourishing over the internet and emulating the music with their own flare.

Thus, a sub-genre was born.
